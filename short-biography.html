Short Biography

Hi! My name is Sandy. From September 2019 until November 2020, I worked on identifying and tackling the current challenges in the Arabic automatic speech recognition field, at the American University of Beirut. Prior to joining AUB, I worked for ten months on a research project which aspires to optimize the process of constructing the speech corpus of unit selection synthesizers, at the University of Rennes I.
My formal learning path resulted in receiving a bachelor’s degree in Computer Science from the Lebanese University in 2014, and then graduating first in class with a master’s degree in Computer Science from the Lebanese University and the University of Toulouse III in 2016.
The talent which makes me stand out the most at work is my superior analytical and verbal reasoning abilities which enable me to solve problems quite quickly and efficiently. I especially possess remarkable critical thinking, logical reasoning, creative thinking, and communication (spoken; written; and non-verbal) competence. My other practical strengths include being extremely organized; being a fast learner; and being able to self-teach myself many things from scratch.
I am intellectually humble. I approach almost everything in a “learning experience” mindset which often gives rise to innovative personal growth opportunities. For instance, I take attending an unconventional abstract paintings exhibition as a chance to stretch my creative and flexible thinking abilities.
I am gifted with a very friendly personality. I enjoy collaborating with cooperative and open-minded people. Additionally, I acknowledge that every interaction I have in the workplace has an influence on my professional development, which implicitly contributes to lowering the tensions between the different constituents of the hierarchy system which governs most modern organizations.
In respect to technical computer science skills, my strength lies in my ability to exploit publicly available libraries; frameworks; codes; and other resources to solve my problems or to use them as a basis to build my solutions. In addition to that, I comprehend the way programming languages work which facilitates the process of teaching myself new (needed) programming languages and technologies. For more specific technical skills: Python is the main programming language I employed during my research work at the American University of Beirut and the University of Rennes I; I used C++ to implement the compiler during my first year of graduate studies at the Lebanese University; Ubuntu is the only operating system installed on my computer (I installed it); I built my simple mobile-friendly professional website from the ground up; I write my documents in LaTeX; and I consult a search engine when I have a particular technical question.
Besides computer science, I hold other interests such as philosophy; history; human behavior; and art. Therefore, I enjoy spending my free time doing activities which expand my breadth and depth of knowledge in these areas. This precisely takes the form of reading articles and non-fiction books which cover a topic(s) of interest; watching informative documentaries; frequenting art museums and galleries; and visiting archeological sites and historic monuments. Furthermore, I am very fond of international affairs which drives me to keep up with global news on a daily basis. Lastly, I enjoy meeting and exchanging ideas with people coming from different cultures and backgrounds which further widen my opinions and conclusions.
